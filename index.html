biography

Born by the Caspian Sea, Iran—the land of Cyrus’s long shadow—I learned to read patterns in sand, wind and water.

I studied at home for my B.Sc., wandered to Isfahan’s blue domes for my M.Sc., and then north to Finland, where Aalto became a compass. Under the guidance of Arkady Krasheninnikov, Hannu-Pekka Komsa, Matti Puska, and Tapio Ala-Nissilä, I learned to listen to materials. With Kari Laasonen's group, I traced the quiet chemistry of electrons and reactions.

What began as a hobby—machine learning, quantum mechanics, electronic-structure calculations, data and code—became my craft: building models that let matter speak, and turning whispers of theory into tools for energy, materials, and the small revolutions that begin inside a simulation.
